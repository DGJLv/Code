Desserts

"Stressed Spelled Backwards is Desserts"

- Loretta LaRoche -

tart

an open pastry case with a filling, usually of something sweet such as fruit

waffle

a type of bread or cake made from batter (= a thin mixture of milk, flour, and egg) cooked in a special pan whose surface forms a pattern of raised squares

tiramisu

an Italian dessert consisting of layers of sponge cake soaked in coffee and brandy or liqueur with powdered chocolate and mascarpone cheese

cookie

a small sweet cake, typically round and flat and having a crisp or chewy texture

cupcake

a small cake baked in a cup-shaped container and typically iced

eclair

a small, soft, log-shaped pastry filled with cream and typically topped with chocolate icing

macaron

a small round cake with a meringue-like consistency, made with egg whites, sugar, and powdered almonds and consisting of two halves sandwiching a creamy filling

mousse

a sweet or savory dish made as a smooth light mass with whipped cream and beaten egg white, flavored with chocolate, fish, etc., and typically served chilled

crepe

a thin pancake

souffle

a light, spongy baked dish made typically by adding flavored egg yolks to stiffly beaten egg whites

financier

a small almond cake, flavoured with beurre noisette, usually baked in a small mold, light and moist with a crisp, eggshell-like exterior, the traditional financier also contains egg whites, flour, and powdered sugar. The molds are usually small rectangular loaves similar in size to petits fours.

chou

a choux pastry, usually in the form of a small round cake with a sweet filling

canele

a small pastry flavored with rum and vanilla with a soft and tender custard center and a dark, thick caramelized crust

tuile

a thin curved cookie, typically made with almonds
